Camp Philosophy

You currently ride 160km/week. This camp takes you to approximately 3× your weekly volume in a periodised structure: hard days are genuinely hard, easy days protect your legs, and coffee rides keep the joy in cycling. The week follows a classic 3:1 load-to-recovery ratio.

Hard — Threshold / climbing Medium — Endurance Easy — Recovery / coffee Rest — Active recovery

── Week One · Building the Base

Easy · Coffee Ride

Legs Openers — Coastal Loop South

Start gently. Head south from Los Gigantes along the TF-47 coast road through Puerto de Santiago and Playa de San Juan, down to Alcalá. Keep the effort conversational — this is about moving your legs after travel, not training. Enjoy the La Gomera views across the channel. Keep heart rate in Zone 1–2 throughout.

Key Roads

TF-47 coastal road (quiet, smooth, flat-ish)

Effort

Zone 1–2 only. Chat pace. No surges. This is a leg-opener.

Duration

~1.5–2 hrs. Stop for coffee mid-ride. No rush.

02
Medium · Endurance

Tamaimo Climb + Teno Loop

65km

Distance

1,100m

Elevation

▶
☕ Coffee at Bar Soto, Santiago del Teide

Your first proper climb. Head north out of Los Gigantes on the TF-454 toward Tamaimo — 5.8km at 7% average, a proper Category 2 effort. Reach Santiago del Teide for coffee, then swing west into the Teno mountains toward Erjos and El Tanque before looping back south via the coast. Steady endurance pace — this establishes your aerobic base for the week.

Route

Los Gigantes → Tamaimo (TF-454) → Santiago del Teide → Erjos → El Tanque → Los Silos → coast south → Los Gigantes

Key Climb

Tamaimo: 5.8km / 7% avg — Cat 2. Steady effort, keep something in reserve.

Effort

Zone 2–3. Climbs at solid tempo, descents and flats at easy endurance.

Notes

First real climb — don't smash it. Bring a light jacket for the descent.

03
Hard · Big Climb

Teide from the West — TF-38 Ascent

90km

Distance

2,100m

Elevation

▶
☕ Coffee at Bar Parada, Chío — legendary cycling café

The signature route from Los Gigantes. Head north through Tamaimo and Santiago del Teide, then follow the TF-38 all the way to the Teide high point at 2,078m. This is 36.6km of climbing gaining 2,068m at 5.6% average — one of the longest sustained climbs in Europe. Stop at Bar Parada in Chío before the final push. Descend back to Los Gigantes via the same route.

Route

Los Gigantes → Tamaimo → Santiago del Teide → Chío → TF-38 → Teide high point → descend same route

The Climb

36.6km total / 2,068m gain / 5.6% avg. Quiet road, good surface. Very little traffic.

Effort

Climb at Zone 3–4. Find a sustainable rhythm early. Last 5km gets tough.

Gear

Gilet or jacket for descent mandatory. Arm warmers. At least 3 bottles or refill at Chío.

04
Easy · Recovery

Active Recovery — Harbour Spin

30km

Distance

300m

Elevation

▶
☕ Coffee at harbour café, Los Gigantes — linger as long as you like

Yesterday was big. Today is about flushing your legs and protecting your form for the second half of the camp. Ride south along the coast to Playa San Juan and back, keeping everything Zone 1. No climbs, no effort, no heroics. This is where amateurs make mistakes by going too hard — trust the process.

Route

Los Gigantes → Puerto de Santiago → Playa San Juan → return. Flat as possible.

Effort

Zone 1 only. If you can't hold a conversation, you're going too hard.

Recovery Protocol

Post-ride: legs up for 20 mins. Sea swim if possible. Foam roll quads & calves.

Optional

Skip the bike and swim/walk instead. Real recovery sometimes means no saddle.

05
Hard · Epic Loop

Masca + North Coast Grand Tour

105km

Distance

2,200m

Elevation

▶
☕ Coffee at Buenavista del Norte — town square café

The most spectacular day of the camp. Climb to Tamaimo and Santiago del Teide, then continue to Mirador de Masca. Descend into Masca village (650m altitude) — the descent is technical and hair-raising — then climb out the other side (3.8km at 11.5% avg — a World Top 25 epic climb). Continue to El Palmar and Buenavista del Norte for coffee, then return via El Tanque and Santiago del Teide. A full day in the saddle.

Route

Los Gigantes → Santiago del Teide → Masca descent → Masca climb → El Palmar → Buenavista → El Tanque → Erjos → Santiago del Teide → Los Gigantes

Key Climb

Masca: 3.8km / 11.5% avg / 430m gain. Brutal and beautiful. One of the most epic climbs in the world.

Effort

Masca at Zone 4–5. Long endurance on the rest. Eat and drink early — this is a long day.

Notes

Start early (7–7:30am). The Masca descent is technical — brake early, watch for gravel.

Protocol

▶

A full rest day between the two training blocks is non-negotiable. You've covered roughly 375km and 6,600m of climbing in 6 days — your body needs this. Walk to the cliffs viewpoint, take the boat trip to see the 600m vertical walls from the sea, or just eat, sleep, and stretch. Stay off the bike completely. This is where the adaptation happens.

09
Hard · Teide Repeat

Teide Full Loop — West Up, South Down

115km

Distance

2,400m

Elevation

▶
☕ Coffee at Bar Parada, Chío + Visitor Centre at summit

The big loop. Climb Teide via the western TF-38, then continue over the summit plateau and descend the southern side toward Vilaflor and Arona, sweeping back along the TF-82 to the coast. You'll cross multiple climate zones — sub-tropical coast, pine forest, lunar lava fields, and the volcanic crater plateau. One of the greatest cycling experiences in Europe.

Route

Los Gigantes → Tamaimo → Chío → TF-38 → Teide plateau → Vilaflor → Arona → Los Cristianos → coastal TF-47 north → Los Gigantes

Key Stats

TF-38 ascent: 36.6km / 2,068m. Total loop is a genuine all-day epic. Allow 5–7 hours.

Effort

Pace the climb at Zone 3 — you have 115km total. The plateau is cold and windy. Descend carefully.

Gear

Full kit for summit: gilet, arm warmers, knee warmers, gloves. 4+ bottles or hydration pack.

Masca + Teide Double — The Camp Finale

Distance

▶

The queen stage. Climb to Santiago del Teide, descend to Masca and climb back out (that 11.5% wall again), continue north to Buenavista, swing back through El Tanque and Erjos, then chain across to Chío and begin the Teide ascent on TF-38 to the high point. Two of Tenerife's greatest climbs in one day. This is what training camp is about.

Effort

Masca at Zone 4. Teide at Zone 3 — you'll be tired. Nutrition is everything today.

Nutrition

Start eating at 30 mins. Gel or bar every 45 mins. Don't wait to feel hungry — you won't catch up.

Pro Camp Nutrition

Eat Like You Mean It

At this volume, nutrition is training. Under-eating is the most common mistake at cycling camps. The targets below are for hard days — adjust down by 15–20% on easy and rest days.

Hard Day Calories

3,200–3,800

kcal total. More for 100km+ days with big elevation.

Carbohydrate Target

8–10g/kg

Body weight per day. Carbs are your fuel — don't fear them.

Protein Target

1.8–2.2g/kg

Per day for muscle repair. Spread across all meals.

On-Bike Fuelling

60–90g carbs per hour while riding. Gels, bars, rice cakes, bananas. Start at 30 mins in — don't wait.

Hydration

500–750ml per hour on the bike. Add electrolyte tabs — you're sweating in Tenerife's heat. 2–3L total on big days.

Recovery Window

Within 30 mins of finishing: 60–80g carbs + 20–30g protein. Chocolate milk, rice + eggs, or recovery shake.

Easy Day & Rest Day Adjustment

On easy days, reduce overall calorie intake by ~15–20% and cut back on simple carbs. Prioritise protein and vegetables. On the full rest day, eat intuitively — your hunger will drop naturally. Don't under-eat protein on rest days: that's when repair happens.
