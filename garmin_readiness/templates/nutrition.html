How This Plan Works

THE PRINCIPLES

🥩
Protein First

160–180g daily protects muscle over 50. Every meal anchored to a protein source. GetPro at lunch on Mon/Wed/Thu closes the gap from the office food choices.

🔋
Carbs Around Training

Rice and pasta lunches on training and ride days. Banana pre-session. Paella Thursday fuels evening kettlebell. For the early weekend starts, the carb-rich Saturday dinner — not breakfast — fuels Sunday's long ride.

💷
Budget Asda

Just Essentials chicken, Asda own rice, Ben's Paella as the one brand treat. £49/week standard, £35/week with batch cooking and skyr swap.

🍳
Gousto Dinners

Dinners not specified here — planned separately via Gousto. Training days: aim 50–55g P, 680 kcal. Rest days: 44g P, 600 kcal. Thursday especially needs a strong pick.

☀️
Sunday = Max Fuel

Carb-rich Saturday dinner is the real pre-ride meal. 100–150 kcal fast carbs on waking. Fuel on-bike from minute 0 at 60–90 g/hr. The big porridge breakfast becomes the post-ride recovery meal — chocolate milk first.

≥2 h baseline — scales up

Long-ride calories scale with duration. The ~2,350 kcal baseline assumes a ~2 h ride. Add 150–200 kcal per ride-hour beyond 2 h, taken mostly as on-bike carbs: ~2,350 at 2 h · ~2,700 at 3.5 h · ~3,000+ at 5 h. A 5 h Z2 ride burns ~2,500–3,000 kcal — fixing Sunday at 2,350 puts you 1,500+ kcal down on your biggest day and blunts the very adaptations weeks 9–11 exist to build.

Gut training: the gut is trainable, and 60–90 g/hr only works if you rehearse it. Weeks 5–8, practise 60 g/hr on every ride ≥75 min. Weeks 9–11, push to 75–90 g/hr on the long rides so the stomach is ready for Tenerife and the 190 km charity day. Use your exact race-day mix now — no new products on event day.

Recovery Week (W4): Drop targets by ~150–200 kcal. 1–2 snacks instead of 3. Lighter Gousto picks. Protein holds at 160g — in a calorie deficit protein must hold or rise; take the cut from carbs and fat only.

COST OPTIMISER

Four swaps take the weekly bill from £49.44 down to ~£35. All protein targets remain achievable. The skyr and batch-chicken swaps are the biggest wins.

Batch cook tip: Roast 1kg chicken thighs Sunday at 200°C / 25 min. Season with mixed herbs. Cool and portion into 3 × 240g lunch containers + 150g Sunday recovery pot. Keeps 4 days in fridge. Cook a second batch Wednesday for Fri if needed.

Skyr portioning: Buy 2 × Arla Skyr 450g pots Sunday. Spoon into 8 small containers (~112g each). Each portion delivers 15g protein — identical to GetPro. Add a spoonful of fruit for variety. Takes 5 minutes.
