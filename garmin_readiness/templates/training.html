THE SADDLE

A 6-day multi-discipline programme for returning cyclists over 50. Three bike sessions (Zone 2 base + a long Sunday ride), two kettlebell & MaxiClimber sessions for posterior chain strength, and a progressive Saturday ruck for low-impact aerobic conditioning. Monday off — every week.

Why this structure works: Strength on Tue/Thu front-loads the week's heavy lifting; Wed/Fri rides keep legs spinning at Zone 2 (high-cadence work is actively restorative); Saturday's ruck is aerobic and load-bearing without the cycling-specific fatigue; Sunday's long ride is the cornerstone aerobic stimulus. Monday rest lets you absorb the previous week before starting again.

Training Zones — Heart Rate & Power

KNOW YOUR ZONES

These zones are based on your Max HR of 191 bpm (your measured value, not an age estimate) and FTP (Functional Threshold Power). Do an FTP test in Week 3 once you have baseline fitness. Until then, use the HR ranges and perceived effort.

Z1
Active Recovery
HR: <68% max
<130 bpm
Power: <55% FTP
Spin easy. Flush legs. The default for short midweek bike spins.
Z2
Endurance
HR: 68–83% max
130–158 bpm
Power: 55–75% FTP
Your fat-burning engine. Most of this plan lives here.
Z3
Tempo
HR: 83–94% max
158–180 bpm
Power: 76–90% FTP
Comfortably hard. Introduced conservatively in Phase 3.
Z4
Threshold
HR: 94–100% max
180–191 bpm
Power: 91–105% FTP
Hard but controlled. Used only for FTP tests.
Z5
VO2 Max
HR: >100% — max effort
Power: >106% FTP
Not used in this programme. Too high a recovery cost early on.

Over-50 note: Heart rate recovery slows with age. If your HR is climbing at steady power during a ride, back off — this is a sign of cumulative fatigue. Trust the HR ceiling, not the power number alone. Same principle applies on the ruck — if HR drifts up at the same pace, slow down.

Volume Overview

PROGRESSIVE LOAD

Total weekly training time (bike + strength + ruck) builds across three phases with scheduled recovery weeks every 4th week. The 3-week build / 1-week recovery pattern is especially important for over-50s, where recovery time increases with age and total training stress is high. Pay attention to how you feel — these are target volumes, not commandments.

THE PROGRAMME

Foundation Phase. The goal is to get back to consistent training across all three disciplines and build the habit. Every session should feel easy to moderate. Bike rides are 100% Zone 1–2. Strength is technique-focused, not max effort. Rucking starts gentle — short distances at conversational pace. No ego sessions.

Supporting Your Training

OVER-50 ESSENTIALS

🏋️
Kettlebell Smart, Not Heavy

For over-50 cyclists, the goal of KB work is posterior chain strength and movement quality, not max load. Stick to 6–12 reps with weights you can move with perfect form. Two non-negotiables: hinge from hips on swings/deadlifts (never round the back), and never sacrifice form for one more rep. Heavy and ugly is how injuries happen.

🥾
Rucking Form

Pack weight should sit high on your back (between shoulder blades, not on your lower back). Walk tall, slight forward lean from ankles not hips. Avoid heel-striking hard — try a midfoot landing. Downhill is where joints take a hit; slow down deliberately and use trekking poles if you have knee history.

🍽️
Fuelling for Fat Loss

Don't under-eat to lose weight faster — it backfires, especially with 6 training days. Eat 30–40g of carbs per hour on rides over 75 minutes. Protein at every meal (1.6–2g/kg bodyweight daily) is essential to preserve muscle while losing fat over 50 — even more critical now you're strength training.

😴
Sleep is Non-Negotiable

Over-50s need 7–9 hours. Recovery hormone production peaks during deep sleep, and you're now stressing three different systems (cycling, strength, load-bearing). Skimping on sleep will plateau your progress and increase injury risk more than any training error. Prioritise it like a session.

💧
Hydration

Thirst response diminishes with age. Drink 500ml before a ride or ruck, 500–750ml per hour while active, and keep drinking after. Add electrolytes on sessions over 90 minutes — rucks count too. Sweat losses from a hot summer ruck can be substantial.

🦵
Knee & Joint Care

Saddle height correct (slight bend at bottom of pedal stroke). Spin lighter gears rather than grinding. Watch knee position on KB squats — knees track over toes, never collapsing inward. If knees ache post-session, ice 10 minutes and flag to a physio — don't ride or ruck through joint pain.

⚖️
Managing 6-Day Training

This is a high training-stress programme. The non-negotiable rule: never skip Monday rest, even if you feel fine. If you're carrying fatigue mid-week, swap a strength session for mobility/yoga rather than push through. Two consecutive days of feeling "off" is a signal to take an extra rest day — better than getting injured or sick.

📈
Why Zone 2 for Weight Loss

At Zone 2 intensity, your body burns a higher proportion of fat as fuel than at higher intensities. The long Sunday rides are especially powerful: after 90+ minutes, fat becomes the dominant fuel source. The strength work builds the engine; the Zone 2 hours burn the fuel. Consistency here outperforms any high-intensity approach.
